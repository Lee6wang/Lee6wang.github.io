# What I Thought About in 2025

## 1 Introduction

Today is December 26, 2025. There are four days left before the year ends.

Recently, I have frequently fallen into long periods of thinking:
some of it is about technology, some is about choices, and some is only about -
**"what exactly am I doing?"**

These thoughts do not always lead to clear conclusions, but they appear again and again.
Rather than letting them spin around in my head, I might as well write them down,
as a staged review and also as an account of this year.

## 2 Change

I choose the word "change" to begin this chapter not to make it sound grand,
but because I truly feel that:

**The me of 2025 and the me of 2024 are no longer at the same level of thinking.**

This change is not reflected in how many earth-shaking things I did,
but more in subtle yet continuous shifts:

- My first reaction when facing problems
- The way I understand tools, processes, and efficiency
- And a renewed understanding of "my position inside the system"

But this change did not begin at the start of the year.

The first half of 2025 was still familiar and steady for me:
running simulations and experiments in the lab, occasionally writing code;
planting crops in *Stardew Valley*, and pushing forward some "things that should be done" in daily life.

On the surface, everything was moving forward.
But looking back, it is hard to say that I had formed judgments and summaries that truly belonged to me.

The real turning point happened in August.

During that period, I went to Hangzhou and participated in AdventureX.
In a sense, that was the first time I truly walked out of my comfort zone.

I have always considered myself an introverted person,

There, for the first time, I came into such dense contact with people from different backgrounds:
researchers from universities such as CMU and MIT;
young entrepreneurs already building companies in Shenzhen, whose eyes lit up when they talked about the future;
and people much younger than me, some who had just finished junior high or high school,
yet already had quite mature development ability.

## 3 AI Today

Three years have passed since ChatGPT appeared.
I think very few people would still deny the role of AI in life and work.

For me, this influence is very concrete, especially in the way I develop.

If I went back to 2024, my SOP for writing code was roughly:

> Write a prompt -> submit it to AI -> get a piece of code that may not be usable -> debug repeatedly

By 2025, this process had gradually evolved into:

> Write a prompt -> submit it to AI (Agent) -> get code with clear structure and relatively high quality -> do a small amount of debugging

This change is not simply "improved efficiency."
It made me truly realize for the first time that:

**Human labor is rapidly losing its former irreplaceability in front of machines.**

Even in the embedded field that I am familiar with -
a direction once considered to have high professional barriers and strong dependence on experience and details -
those barriers are being continuously weakened with the help of AI.

This realization is not exciting.
Instead, it comes with a hard-to-ignore uneasiness.

## 4 How I Should Adjust Myself

I thought about this question for a long time.

To be honest, I once had a strong sense of crisis:

**If the threshold for acquiring technology keeps dropping,
will the learning achievements I accumulated over many years be easily caught up with, or even surpassed?**

But after thinking about it repeatedly, I gradually realized:
perhaps the question is not "will I be surpassed,"
but rather -

**whether I still build my value on "completing a specific technical action."**

One thing 2025 slowly taught me is:
AI is not replacing "thinking."
It is replacing **repetitive labor at the execution level**.

If a person only:

- Writes code
- Builds modules
- Runs processes

then replacement is almost foreseeable.

But if a person can participate more in:

- Designing the problem itself
- Building system structure
- Judging tradeoffs, risks, and boundaries
- Integrating scattered technologies into sustainable solutions

then AI becomes an amplifier rather than a threat.

So I began to consciously adjust my focus:

- From "how to write it" to "why design it this way"
- From "single-point technology" to "systems and structure"
- From "finishing tasks" to "forming reusable methodology"

This is not a process that can be completed overnight,
but at least I am already standing in a different position.

## 5 Final Words

Looking back on 2025, I do not think it was a year of "explosive results."
But it may well be a year when my **way of thinking changed fundamentally**.

I began to realize that growth does not necessarily appear as increased speed.
Sometimes what truly matters is whether the direction has shifted.

Perhaps one day in the future, when I look back at this year,
I will find that my thoughts at this moment were still immature.
But at least at this moment, I am willing to admit:

**I am no longer merely moving forward passively.
I am trying to understand why I am moving forward.**

If you also happen to be standing at a similar stage,
I hope this text can bring a little companionship and resonance.
